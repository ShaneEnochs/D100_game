# Build Backlog

The construction work the module requires between acts, with specifications
and anchors. Do each through the content-authoring and monster-building
skills; base stats always come from `statblock.py`.

## Before the Act 3 climax (target: during Act 2 play)

**1. Level 6-8 creature blocks.** The Anchor Valley leaks infernals in
waves as the array warms. Build a small set (4-6 creature types) of
*(Infernal)* entries in the 6-8 band, bestiary format, each with a
mechanic that touches the portal/mana layer (the Rift Whelp's Portal
Tether is the pattern: an open portal replaces losses, so the fight
rewards objective play over attrition). Wave composition should scale —
early waves from the 5-6 band, late waves 7-8.

**2. The Shade Courier.** Tier 1 high-level infernal (module: "GM builds
the block from the monster tables before its first encounter" — level 8-10
fits "high-level Tier 1"). Design intent from the module: it does not fight
if it can leave; it fights only to protect the array's final stabilization.
Give it abilities that serve withdrawal and observation (concealment,
disengage, a screen), not a damage kit. Its first possible appearance is
quest S-13 — build it BEFORE running S-13.

**3. Bett Sorrel, statted human.** The crew's only real combat problem,
"level 5-ish," a washed-out hunter. Humans use the four-stat PC model, so
build her as a character, not a monster: stats around a trained level-5
hunter (one primary stat in the 40s), 2 skill slots + 1 aspect from the
catalog (she is Tier 1), real equipment from equipment.md. She appears in
the survey-patrol ambush, possibly S-18, and the valley battle — hard,
survivable, costly.

**4. Kade's crew hands.** Four to six hirelings, statted light: treat as
level 2-4 humans with simple weapons; one block reused. Kade himself is
"mid-Tier-1 personally" — a level 4-5 human with Social/Intellect leaning,
dangerous through preparation, not arms.

**5. The array disruption task chain.** The climax needs the mechanic
written before the battle: a chain of Intellect/Magic checks under fire,
with defined bonuses for the Delve knowledge, Grint's counter-device (S-5,
+15 on readings), Valin's help, and the keystone extraction as the final
physical step. Define the check count, difficulties, and what each failed
round costs (a worse wave). Write it down like a monster block — before
initiative, never during.

## Before Act 4

**6. Tier 2 bestiary.** Front-line infernals and contested-ground
creatures, levels ~6-15. Follow the bestiary voice and format; Tier 2 base
stats from `statblock.py 11`-`statblock.py 15` and up. Include
portal-nexus garrison types and at least one leader-creature pattern.
Note the Tier 2 catalog gap too: PCs at 11+ get a third skill slot and
second aspect slot, and skills_and_aspects.md currently tops out at the
level 8 band — the level 10+ catalog rows are new design (costs from the
core XP table: level 10 = 550, 15 = 1,070, 20 = 1,790).

**7. Fort Greywatch and the Ninth.** New home base and cast: Captain
Isolde Vane, Senior Hunter Calla Wren (introduce her earlier via S-22 if
possible), plus the garrison texture the world file gave Fort Easy Dawn —
routine, places, support staff. Each named NPC gets an `npc_knowledge`
entry starting from ignorance before their first line.

**8. Operation frames as runnable material.** The module lists five frames
(portal response, line defense at Bell's Ford, deep patrol, evacuation,
strike). For each, prepare the same skeleton: objective, opposition from
the Tier 2 bestiary, terrain notes, the clock, and what the war layer
learns from success or failure.

**9. The second Tithe cell.** Act 4's arc needs its own Kade-equivalents.
Keep the pattern (skim → move → bury → mask) but vary the masking — the
front's own portal noise is the stated cover. Track cell knowledge in
`npc_knowledge` from day one.
